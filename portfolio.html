How is this actually done?

I'm sure we've all heard about a variety of goal planning methodologies, the means to make our 'greatest dreams happen' which as experience can suggest is often met with unfulfilled satisfaction from the lofty expectation that all I had to do was make the goal and 'work at it'

So what actually happened then? What separates the conditions of this?

It is not enough to just make the attempt, to put in the time, or to make it the gym. While those are apart of the system itself, they are only a step in the process, not the process itself.

Optimizing Our Flow

So, how does one simply 'optimize' their flow? Well for conveniency, we broke it down into a few steps, to make life simple of course!

1. time

2. place

3. preparation

4. needs

5. consistently making that the place to get things done.

6. breaks (the promodoro effect)

7. rinse, repeat and evaluate

Simply put, we must plan to make the 'perfect effort' into the step of the system, not just the act of attempting. This is the defining label between trying and doing.
